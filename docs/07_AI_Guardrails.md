# AI guardrails

## Core AI doctrine

AI is an internal advisory layer.
AI must help the team work faster without becoming the decision-maker.

## Allowed AI roles

### Guidance
- indicator interpretation help
- evidence suggestions
- missing-gap suggestions
- recurring cadence suggestions
- work-plan suggestions

### Drafting
- SOP draft
- checklist draft
- minute template draft
- compliance note draft
- filename suggestion
- print-pack ordering suggestion

### Assessment
- evidence sufficiency recommendation
- review-readiness recommendation
- final assessment recommendation

## Disallowed AI roles

AI must not:
- approve indicators
- submit indicators
- directly change status
- overwrite human-entered records silently
- auto-close gaps
- replace reviewer/approver judgment
- invent evidence that does not exist
- claim inspection readiness as a binding truth

## Human review requirement

Any AI output that influences workflow must be:
- visible as AI-generated
- stored with traceability
- manually reviewed
- manually accepted or rejected

## Manual AI preferred mode

Preferred initial mode:
- manual AI assistance
- human-triggered drafting and assessment
- human copy/review/accept process

Automated AI API integration should be deferred until:
- the workflow is stable
- the contract is stable
- the value clearly justifies the added operational risk

## Drift risks AI can cause

AI may drift the product into:
- generic content generation
- excessive automation
- fake readiness confidence
- paperwork without evidence trace
- over-reliance on soft-copy output
- weak governance

The system design must prevent this.
